About BRCA

BRCA1 and BRCA2 are two genes that are important to fighting cancer. They are tumor suppressor genes. When they work normally, these genes help keep breast, ovarian, and other types of cells from growing and dividing too rapidly or in an uncontrolled way.

What your Result means

  • POSITIVE RESULT

    A positive test result means that someone has a mutation in one of the breast cancer genes, BRCA1 or BRCA2, but it does not mean they are certain to develop cancer. Follow-up care after a positive test result should include modifying screening for cancer and considering procedures and medications to reduce risk, depending on factors such as age, medical history, prior treatments, past surgeries, and personal preferences.

  • NEGATIVE OR UNCERTAIN TEST

    Negative test results do not guarantee breast cancer risk. The BRCA gene test can detect the majority of mutations in the BRCA1 and BRCA2 genes, but it may not be able to detect a high-risk gene mutation. Other genetic tests can detect other hereditary cancer gene mutations.
